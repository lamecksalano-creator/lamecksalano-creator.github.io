What Selling at Samsung Taught Me About Customer Trust

By Lameck Salano

Working in sales at Samsung Ltd taught me that customers rarely buy a product on specs alone — they buy because they trust the person recommending it. I learned to slow down, actually listen to what a customer needed the device for, and only then match them to the right option. That approach consistently outperformed pushing the newest or most expensive model. In this piece, I share the specific habits that built trust on the sales floor, from honest trade-off conversations to following up after the sale rather than disappearing once the receipt was printed.

Why the Giraffe Centre Is More Than a Nairobi Tourist Stop

By Lameck Salano

As a Tour Guide at the Giraffe Centre, I watched first-time visitors arrive expecting a quick photo opportunity and leave with a genuine understanding of Rothschild's giraffe conservation. The centre's work goes far beyond the feeding platform tourists know it for — it's tied directly to breeding and reintroduction efforts that have helped pull this giraffe subspecies back from the edge. Here I walk through what a visit actually involves, why the timing of your visit matters, and how the centre's conservation work connects to the wider picture of wildlife protection in Kenya.

Bringing Conservation Education Into the Classroom

By Lameck Salano

Coordinating conservation education with the Wildlife Clubs of Kenya, in partnership with KWS and NEMA, showed me how much of conservation work actually happens outside the field — in classrooms, community halls, and youth clubs. Getting students to care about wildlife protection isn't about lecturing them on statistics; it's about connecting local ecosystems to things they already care about. I break down some of the approaches that worked best in my experience, and why youth-focused conservation education is one of the most underrated tools in Kenya's conservation strategy.
